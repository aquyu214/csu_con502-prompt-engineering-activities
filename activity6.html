Activity – AI for CM Research Prompt Competition

Research Scenario

A first-year Construction Management (CM) graduate student wants to use AI tools to support their research but does not know exactly how AI can help during each stage of the research process.

Your challenge is to design a prompt that will make an AI model generate a structured, document-style guide on how CM graduate students can use AI responsibly and effectively throughout the research lifecycle.

Your Task

Use any prompt engineering techniques you'd like to create an optimized prompt, then use it to generate a clear, structured document-style guide that covers:

  • Planning research topics and refining research questions
  • Developing search strings and summarizing literature
  • Designing surveys and interview protocols
  • Qualitative coding support and conceptual statistics explanations
  • Drafting, revising, and organizing academic writing
  • Ethical and responsible AI use in CM research
  • Construction-specific examples (safety, scheduling, BIM, productivity)

Your final output should look like a mini document or research-support guide.

You may use:

  • Zero-shot prompting
  • One-shot or few-shot prompting
  • Role prompting
  • Chain-of-Thought prompting
  • Contextual prompting
  • Automatic Prompt Engineering (APE)
  • Prompt rewriting or mutation
  • Any structured or creative prompting strategy you prefer

Start with a Simple Prompt

Begin with something basic, such as:

This starting prompt is intentionally vague. Your goal is to improve it dramatically using prompt engineering.

Instructions

  • Start with the simple prompt provided above.
  • Use any prompt-engineering techniques to improve it.
  • Find your best optimized prompt.
  • Use that optimized prompt to generate a document-style guide.
  • Copy the final guide into a Word document (.docx).
  • Export your Word document as a PDF file.
  • Submit the PDF version only for class voting.
