Activity – AI for CM Research Prompt Competition

Research Scenario

A first-year Construction Management (CM) graduate student wants to use AI tools to support their research but does not know exactly how AI can help during each stage of the research process.

Your challenge is to design and use a prompt workflow that guides an AI model to produce a structured, document-style guide on how CM graduate students can use AI responsibly and effectively throughout the research lifecycle.

Your Task

Use any prompt engineering techniques you'd like. Your workflow may include:

  • a single well-crafted prompt,
  • a sequence of prompts that build on each other,
  • follow-up prompts that refine or expand earlier outputs,
  • revisions, rewrites, critiques, or improvements,
  • different prompts for different parts of the document.

In other words, the final document does not need to come from one prompt. Use whatever iterative process helps you produce the best result.

The final guide should address:

  • Planning research topics and refining research questions
  • Developing search strings and summarizing literature
  • Designing surveys or interview protocols
  • Support for qualitative and conceptual quantitative analysis
  • Drafting and revising academic writing
  • Ethical and responsible AI use in research
  • Construction-specific examples (safety, scheduling, BIM, productivity)

The final output should read like a short research-support document.

You may use:

  • Zero-shot prompting
  • One-shot or few-shot prompting
  • Role prompting
  • Chain-of-Thought prompting
  • Contextual prompting
  • Automatic Prompt Engineering (APE)
  • Prompt critique, rewriting, or mutation
  • Any iterative workflow you prefer

Start with a Simple Prompt

Begin with something basic, such as:

This starting prompt is intentionally vague. Use multiple prompts (improvements, critiques, expansions) to refine it or replace it.

Instructions

  • Start with the simple prompt provided above.
  • Use as many prompts as you need to refine, improve, and tailor your approach.
  • When satisfied, generate the final document-style guide.
  • Copy the final guide into a Word document (.docx).
  • Format it clearly (titles, bullets, sections).
  • Export your Word document as a PDF.
  • Submit the PDF version only for class voting.

Note:
AI tools can support your research workflow, but they may generate incorrect or unverified information. Always apply critical evaluation and follow academic integrity guidelines.
